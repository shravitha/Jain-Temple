Mutt and Parampara

Parampara

The Moodabidri Gurupeetha (Religious seat) belongs to the Acharya Kunda indigenous tradition and the titular name of the religious head is like this: “Srimad Delli Kanakadri Swethapura Sudhapura Sangeethapura, Srimad Belagola, Kshemavenupura and Sidha Simhasanadheeswara”, Bhattaraka is said to mean a scholar who has mastered all branches of knowledge, arts with unquenching thirst for knowledge. In other words, the Bhattaraka is expected to be well-versed in religious knowledge, knowledgeable through much reading, well-wisher of monks and Great Souls’ and he should propagate religious principles.

The present Gurupeetha was founded in 1220 by the then Swasthisri Charukeerthi Pandithacharyavarya of Shravana Belagola Mutt. The king, Bittideva of Dwara Samudra (Hale-Beedu) [1104-1141] abandoned Jainism and accepted the Vaishnavait cult and was renamed as Vishnuvardhana. After this conversion he demolished several basadis and got many a shravaka killed; as a result of this irreligious sinful act there was a split in this part of the earth; men and animals lost their lives. Veera Ballal, the King that ruled this region, between 1173 and 1219 approached the Swmiji of Shravana Belagola and appealed to him to prevent this massacre; in reply to this prayer the Swamiji visited Dwara Samudra (The present Adagooru at Halebeedu) and offered a worship to Shri Bhagwan Parshwanatha Swamy according to the prevalent rites and set the split aright by means of a cucumber charged with some magical power; the split earth came back to its normal shape.

As a result of this supernatural act life in this region returned to normalcy, with sufficient rains and harvests and the people breathed a sigh of relief. Peace and non-violence pervaded the land. The then Swamiji of Shravana Belagola was a great soul, a great religious teacher, a great philosopher, held in high reverence. Most of the time he sat in penance. He was a “Truth-Finder”. By way of his gratitude to the Swamiji the king did everything to bring about religious and moral awareness everywhere. The King and his entire ministerial team prostrated before the Swamiji in all reverence and bestowed up on him the title of “CHARUKEERTHI” and the Swamiji came to be known as “Adya Charukeerthi Bhattaraka Pandithacharyavarya Swamiji”. From there the Swamiji directly went to Nalloor in Karkala of the D.K district and founded a Math; from Nalloor the Swamiji proceeded to Moodbidri and founded another Math.

About

Saavira Kambada Temple, also known as the Chandranatha Temple, is a renowned Jain temple in Moodabidri, Karnataka, India. It boasts a remarkable feature of 1000 pillars, making it a significant architectural marvel.
